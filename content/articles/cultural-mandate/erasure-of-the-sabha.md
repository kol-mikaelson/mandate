Every December, Chennai performs its most elaborate act of cultural self-definition. The sabhas open their doors, the auditoriums fill, and the city reassures itself that classical music remains at the centre of its identity.

But attendance figures are not the same as vitality. The Margazhi season of 2024 has been, in several important respects, the most financially successful and artistically conservative in recent memory — a combination that should give pause to anyone who cares about the form's future.

> "The corporate sponsor does not want experimentation. He wants the hits. He wants the artist his clients will recognise. He wants Bhairavi at 9 PM, not a two-hour alapana in Todi at midnight."

## The Sponsorship Paradox

Carnatic music has, over the past decade, acquired a corporate infrastructure that its practitioners could not have imagined a generation ago. Festival slots are sponsored by IT companies, banks, and real estate developers.

The sabha secretaries, perpetually anxious about filling seats and satisfying sponsors, have responded rationally to these incentives. The result is a season calendar that privileges the bankable over the adventurous, the familiar over the searching.
