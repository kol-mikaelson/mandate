Tamil Nadu's Finance Minister, addressing the state assembly's budget session earlier this year, used a phrase that has since circulated widely in policy circles: "cooperative federalism has become a one-way street." The remark was pointed. It was also, by any serious accounting, accurate.

The GST compensation cess — designed to insulate states from revenue losses incurred by subsuming their own taxation powers into the unified GST framework — expired in June 2022. Tamil Nadu's loss from this expiry, conservatively calculated, exceeds ₹14,000 crore annually.

> "A federalism that punishes good governance is not cooperative. It is extractive."

## The Structural Imbalance

Tamil Nadu is a net contributor to the Union pool. For every rupee it contributes in central taxes, it receives back approximately 29 paise in devolution. This ratio has been declining.

When a state like Tamil Nadu cannot fund its own public health infrastructure, its transport networks, or its higher education institutions at the level its tax base should permit, the consequences are felt not just by its residents but by the national economy that depends on its productivity.
